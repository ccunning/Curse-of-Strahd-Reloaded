# Mountain Folk

> **faction** · 3 source files

## Key Facts

- Those opposed to [[NPCs/Strahd von Zarovich|Strahd]] retreated to [[Places/Soldav|Soldav]] ([[Factions/Mountain Folk|Mountain Folk]]), while others stayed in [[Places/Svalich Woods|Svalich Wood]] ([[Factions/Forest Folk|Forest Folk]]).
- A group of druidic descendants who left the druids after being betrayed by them during [[NPCs/Strahd von Zarovich|Strahd]]’s arrival.
- Live separately from [[Factions/Forest Folk|Forest Folk]] and still hold old beliefs, worshipping ancient goddesses of [[Places/Barovia|Barovia]].
- Are not servants of Strahd and are currently active due to their search for something unknown.
- Two factions derived from [[Factions/First Folk|the First Folk]] following a schism during Strahd's invasion.
- They reject the worship of Strahd and instead honor the Ladies Three as their faith.
- Their leader is an elder [[NPCs/Storm Giant|storm giant]] named Burebis who remembers the old ways.
- The split originated from historical conflicts with [[NPCs/Kavan|Kavan]] and [[NPCs/King Dostron|King Dostron]].
- They live in a hidden settlement called [[Places/Soldav|Soldav]], nestled among [[Places/Mount Ghakis|Mount Ghakis]].
- They have no place in modern politics but are protective of their homeland.
- A people who live in [[Places/Balinok Mountains|Balinok Mountains]], particularly near [[Places/Tsolenka Pass|Tsolenka Pass]].
- They are not aligned with each other and view one another suspiciously.
- No direct interest or use for him due to their hidden settlements.
- A tribe of hunter-gatherers native to the [[Places/Balinok Mountains|Balinok Mountains]].
- Have a hidden settlement called Soldav in [[Places/Tsolenka Pass|Tsolenka Pass]].
- Known to Strahd only as a lost and broken group.

## DM Notes

- Their hidden settlement is mentioned as a key destination if players wish to find ways to reverse land corruption.
- They may provide information regarding ancient lore or be targets for infiltration by players.
- Conflict within these groups may be exploited for dramatic effect or factional alliances.
- A meeting with the [[Factions/Mountain Folk|Mountain Folk]] is required to restore the spear stolen by [[NPCs/Kavan|Kavan]].
