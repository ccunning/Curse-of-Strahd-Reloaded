# Gustav Durst

> **NPC** · 2 source files

## Key Facts

- Is a spirit recognizable by name but also described as terrified, guilty, and self-loathing about his role in Elisabeth's vengeance.
- Can be convinced to step aside using skill checks, particularly with reference to past history and emotional empathy.
- A ghostly figure appearing as a poltergeist blocking the players' exit after defeating the flesh mound.
- Part of the Durst family that played a key role in local economy during the valley’s time.
- The manor's current state indicates his past presence, though he is now deceased.
- Elisabeth’s husband and a merchant who was accused of an affair with [[NPCs/Klara|Klara]].
- Was a tragic figure whose death opened the door for Elisabeth's corruption.
- His portrait hangs in the main hall, holding a smiling doll named Rose.
- His life had strong implications for [[Places/Barovia|Barovia]]’s social dynamics.
- Has children named Rosavalda (Rose) and Thornboldt (Thorn).
- Killed by Elisabeth after she discovered his deception.
- His body appears among the dead in the Family Crypts.
- A member of the Durst family, married to Elisabeth.

## DM Notes

- His name appears associated with the cult's activities as depicted in crypts and documents found inside the house.
- Gustav's warnings about 'other servants' ahead; his spirit may affect further narrative decisions in Act I.
- Gustav's death may tie into familial mysteries within the manor.
