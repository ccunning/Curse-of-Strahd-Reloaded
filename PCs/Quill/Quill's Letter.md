Cordial salutations and highest regards,

If these words have met your eyes, then the secret I have carried in silence has finally been laid bare. You see me now not as the man who walked beside you, but as I truly am.

Long ago, the Archmage Mordenkainen saw fit to grant a simple quill the gift of flesh and sentience as a helper and apprentice to his work. When he departed, he said he was embarking on a quest of the upmost exigent importance, and left me behind to watch his tower. With only a piece of amber and a wave, I have not seen him since... a living spell with no master. For well over a year now, in order to try to protect myself and prevent me from turning back to the way which you now see, I have used every ounce of my abjuration capabilities to shield my own existence; terrified that a single stray pulse of magic would unravel the weave of my life. It seems that shield has finally failed.

I ask you now, not as a mere writing instrument, but as the comrade who fought at your backs: Please, do not let this be my end.

Within my belongings is a box lined with lead and amber. I beg you to place my feather inside it immediately and keep my spellbook safe. The amber will insulate my essence, preserving the "memory" of my humanity and protecting the ink of my soul from the suffocating rot. In that box, I am safe; outside of it, I am merely debris and easily snapped.

Friends, I beseech you, carry me with you. When your journey leads you through and beyond these mists, if able, seek out a power mage of renown. One must be capable of the highest arts of Transmutation to be able to give me breath again. Tell them who I was, who my master was, and show them the last page of my spellbook. Tell them that Quill is not merely a tool to be used, but a soul that wishes to return, one that wants to continue weaving the magic of this plane to continue to help you and the world.

I have spent my life helping in this odd land; I must now ask you to protect me in the only way I have left.

Until the ink flows once more,
Quill
